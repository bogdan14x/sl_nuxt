## Design Context

### Users
Soundlog is for independent artists who need a professional web presence but do not have time to design, build, and maintain a personal site. Their core job is to publish music and promote releases, while Soundlog handles the repetitive website upkeep. The interface should prioritize fast setup, clear publishing status, and confidence that links stay current.

### Brand Personality
Bold, eye-catching, brutalist, subtle.

Emotional goals:
- Excitement about launch quality
- Confidence in reliability and professionalism
- Minimal cognitive overhead while editing

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark mode only.

Visual direction should combine high-impact typography and strong compositional choices with restrained, intentional detail. The product should feel premium and sharp without becoming noisy. It should explicitly avoid looking like Spotify in palette, layout language, iconography rhythm, or brand-adjacent visual patterns.

Reference update from `v2_soundlog.pdf`:
- Base the UI on a near-black background with very bright neutral text and grey subtext.
- Primary accent is indigo/violet: `#594DDF`.
- Supporting semantic colors may use:
  - destructive `#EB2C88`
  - alert `#F29100`
  - constructive `#5CEA94`
- Prefer simple flat surfaces, clean dividers, and compact pill/logo treatments over textured or patterned backgrounds.
- Typography direction should feel like a bold grotesk heading system paired with quieter, highly legible section/body text.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with artist confidence: status clarity, publishing certainty, and trustworthy data freshness.
2. Minimize effort: every screen should reduce maintenance work, not add configuration burden.
3. Use bold hierarchy with restraint: strong headlines and structure, subtle supporting detail.
4. Keep interactions direct: clear CTAs, low-friction flows, and no decorative complexity that obscures actions.
5. Stay visually distinct from Spotify: independent identity in color, rhythm, and UI composition.
6. Favor the reference palette: black, near-white, grey, and indigo should do most of the work.
7. Keep backgrounds plain: avoid patterned backdrops and large atmospheric effects unless they serve a product meaning.
